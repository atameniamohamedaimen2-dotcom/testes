History of IQ Testing: From Binet to Modern Assessments

1905 - First IQ Test

French psychologists Alfred Binet and Theodore Simon created the first modern intelligence test to identify students needing educational assistance.

1912 - IQ Score Concept

William Stern introduced the Intelligence Quotient (IQ) concept, calculated as mental age divided by chronological age.

1916 - Stanford-Binet Test

Lewis Terman at Stanford University adapted Binet's test for American use, creating the Stanford-Binet Intelligence Scales.

1939 - Wechsler Tests

David Wechsler developed the Wechsler Adult Intelligence Scale (WAIS), which became the gold standard for IQ testing.

1946 - Mensa Founded

Mensa, the high IQ society, was founded in England, requiring members to score in the top 2% on standardized IQ tests.

2000s - Digital Revolution

Online IQ tests emerged, making cognitive assessment accessible to millions worldwide through digital platforms.

What is an IQ Score?
IQ (Intelligence Quotient) measures cognitive ability relative to others in the same age group. The average IQ in the US is 98, with a standard deviation of 15. This means about 68% of Americans score between 85 and 115.
